$whoami

I come from a professional background in restaurant management and operations, and am currently currently working in IT for a restaurant data company. I have extensive experience with public relations and customer service, as well as the ins and outs of running a business in the restaurant industry.

My first serious venture into the tech world started with building a PC. I spent months researching and learning all about computer hardware. My research sparked an interest in Linux, and it wasn't long after that I began modifying distributions and attempting to automate daily tasks with shell scripting. Linux led to shell scripting, shell scripting led to learning other programming languages, and I soon developed a passion for programming and information security.

I take every opportunity I have to learn more and advance my skill sets. I'm currently focusing on writing Python programs and taking IT courses, and within the next few months I plan on taking the CompTIA Network+ and Security+ exams to solidify my understanding of computer nextworks and security.


Outside of Tech

Outside of programming and information technology I enjoy traveling, learning human languages, and spending time in the outdoors. I spent a few years living in South America, which helped me expand my worldview and develop a love for the Spanish language and South American culture. After reaching native-like fluency in the language, I developed a passion for linguistics and began to translate professionally between English and Spanish and study other languages in my spare time. I learned a good amount of Quechua (Native American Incan language) while living in South America, and someday hope to help in the preservation of the language by gathering and developing digital resources as even proper books on the language are almost impossible to find. I also read Portuguese and have studied Russian, Mandarin, and Arabic to some extent.
